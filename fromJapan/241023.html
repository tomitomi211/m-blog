You wouldn't know it, but Japan is a culture of "constant apologizing" You might think that Japanese people are all well-mannered and polite, but actually, Japan has a culture where people apologize over the smallest things. This apologetic culture is an important tool for maintaining social harmony. Apologizing is a way to avoid conflict, a very Japanese strategy. Especially in crowded places like trains or elevators, you’ll hear "sorry" exchanged for even the tiniest incidents as part of daily life. When sitting down on the train, if you make eye contact with the person next to you, "Sorry!" At a restaurant, even before calling the waiter, "Sorry!"
